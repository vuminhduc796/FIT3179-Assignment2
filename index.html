TRACKING COVID-19 ON THE GLOBAL SCALE


Global Confirmed Cases by Country

This visualisation used choropleth map to indicate the normalized confirmed cases by a million people. The darker color will represent the higher density of coronavirus patients. As can be seen from the graph, although the virus started spreading from Asia, the heaviest effected countries were United States and Brazil. Moreover, due to the fact that China and India are overpopulated, the total cases per a million of people were low. It is interesting to see the diversity of values in Africa, while some countries were maintaining a low number of cases, the other such as Libra and South Africa were struggling to control the spread of this decease. Overall, Europe countries were having relatively high figures. The data is last updated on 24/10/2020 and visualiszed on 27/10/2020. Filter for minimum population was encoded.


Global Confirmed Cases by Continent

This visualisation demonstrates the total cases of each country, encoded with different colors representing the continent. Although in the first graph, a majority of Oceania countries were having high total of cases by million of people, the total confirmed cases remained low. The identitcal trends are also applied for Africa and Europe. Asia and America were having the most confirmed cases due to the dense and high population. Brazil and United States contributed over half on total cases in America while India is currently dominating in Asia. On the other hand, the total number of confirmed cases in Europe is equally divided to many countries, represented as many rectangles with identical areas. Lastly, South Africa were having the most confirmed cases in Africa, followed by Morocco .


The Outcome of Covid-19 by Continent

In this Sankey diagram, we visualised the outcome of Covid-19 decease by continent. This visualisation focused on how each continent encountered Coronavirus. As can be seen from the diagram, America has the highest number of cases, more than half of those are still active. The total of deaths from America is the highest, comparing to other continents. On the other hand, while Asia and Europe have comparable total of cases, Asia is still in the emergency stage as most of the cases are still active. Europe is recovering from the decease, the total number of recovered cases is high, comparing to other continents. The total of death cases globally remaining low as a good sign. Almost half of the confirmed cases resulted in recovery, while more than half of the cases are still active. Lastly, Oceania and Africa are still doing well, stopping the spread of Covid-19.


Gender and Age Group Effects on Covid-19

This visualisation provides the comparison between different age groups and genders on total of infected cases in Australia. It can be observed from the chart that people between 20-39 years old have the highest risk of infection. When it comes to comparison between male and female, although the patterns are quite similar, female is having higher recorded cases than male. Female at 79+ age group had unexpectedly high number of cases.


Race Effects on Covid-19

This visualisation contains 3 pie charts, representing the population, total cases and total deaths respectively. We can see that although White people has the highest percentage in population, their contribute towards total cases and deaths are decreasing. Black/ African and Asian had similar values for all categories.


Top 10 Countries with Most Cases

This area chart represents the growth of Covid-19 cases by the top 10 affected countries. The dataset was collected from March 2020 to September 2020. We can see clearly that the US, India and Brazil are having the highest number of cases respectively. Followed up by Russia and Colombia. It is noticeable that the total number of cases dramatically increased in July 2020. The total recorded cases contributed from the top 10 countries was nearly 23 millions in September.


The Covid-19 Outcome by Country

In this bubble plot, we can see how each country encounters Covid-19, indicated by the total of deaths and the total of recovered cases. Each country is represented as a dot, with encoded size for the total cases and color for continent. A country that does well will have high number of recoveries but low number of deaths. Although Brazil and United States had comparable total cases, Brazil was doing better by having more recovery cases than the US. This graph shows an interesting fact that the death/recovery ratio was not dependent on continents, as the colors are well-mixed in the middle of the graph. It is reasonable that countries with higher number of total cases will have higher number of deaths and recoveries.
